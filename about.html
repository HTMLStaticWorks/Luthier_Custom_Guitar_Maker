Artisanal Roots

It all started in a small basement workshop with a single slab of mahogany.

Founded by Marcus Thorne in 2005, StringCraft Luthier was born from the desire to create instruments that bridge the gap between vintage tradition and modern precision. Marcus spent years studying the acoustic properties of wood, learning how to manipulate vibration to produce the "Thorne Tone" that artists now crave.

Today, our team has grown, but our mission remains the same: every instrument that leaves our workshop must be a testament to the artisan's touch.

The Workshop

Where traditional tools meet precision engineering.

Traditional Edge

We use hand planes, chisels, and scrapers for the final voicing of every soundboard, just as master builders have for centuries.

Modern Accuracy

Custom CNC technology ensures that our neck profiles and bridge placements are accurate to within a thousandth of an inch.

Controlled Environment

Our wood library is kept at a strictly controlled 45% humidity to ensure long-term stability in any climate.

Our Core Philosophy

"The wood tells you what it wants to become. My job is simply to listen and remove everything that isn't a guitar."

Marcus Thorne, Master Luthier

We reject the assembly-line approach to guitar making. We believe in the slow craft—where every joint is hand-fitted and every finish is hand-rubbed. This attention to detail results in an instrument that feels played-in from the very first strum.
